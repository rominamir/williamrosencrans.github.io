About Me

I'm William Rosencrans, a PhD candidate in Biochemistry and Molecular Biophysics at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech). My research centers around mitochondrial biology — especially the processes that regulate mitophagy, mitochondrial calcium flux, and ion channel dynamics. I’m particularly interested in the role of VDAC proteins and transcriptional regulators like KLF4 in neurodegenerative diseases.

Before Caltech, I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Physics with a minor in Biology from Colgate University. During my undergraduate and post-baccalaureate years, I conducted research in chromatin biophysics, ecology, and single-molecule electrophysiology. I later joined the National Institutes of Health (NIH), where I applied single-molecule techniques to study mitochondrial membrane channels like VDACs.

Today, I work at the interface of biophysics, cell biology, and systems neuroscience — with the goal of better understanding how mitochondrial dysfunction contributes to diseases like Parkinson’s and Alzheimer’s.

Outside the lab, I’m passionate about science communication, coffee, and hiking coastal trails.
