About Me

I'm William Rosencrans, a PhD candidate in Biochemistry and Molecular Biophysics at Caltech. My research centers around mitochondrial biology — especially the processes that regulate mitophagy, mitochondrial calcium flux, and ion channel dynamics.

I hold a BA in Physics (with a minor in Biology) from Colgate University. I've conducted research in chromatin biophysics, ecology, and mitochondrial channel behavior at NIH using single-molecule tools.

Today, I work at the interface of biophysics, cell biology, and systems neuroscience — seeking to understand how mitochondrial dysfunction contributes to neurodegenerative disorders like Parkinson’s and Alzheimer’s.

Outside the lab, I enjoy science communication, hiking, and coffee-fueled coding.
